Welcome to the Planets section!

Here, you can explore all the planets of our Solar System as well as some dwarf planets. Each card provides a short description, key facts such as the number of moons, day length, and average temperature, along with an image to help you visualize these amazing worlds. This page is designed to give you a quick and interactive overview of the wonders of our cosmic neighborhood.

Venus

Venus is often called Earth’s “twin” because of its similar size and structure, but it’s far more extreme.
Key Data:
• Moons: 0
• Day length: 243 Earth days
• Average temperature: 464°C
• Atmosphere: Carbon dioxide, sulfuric acid clouds

Moon

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite. Its cratered surface tells a story of ancient impacts and cosmic history.
Key Data:
• Planet: Earth
• Moons: 0
• Day length: 29.5 Earth days
• Temperature: -173°C to +127°C

Earth

Earth is the only known planet to support life. Covered mostly by oceans and rich in biodiversity.
Key Data:
• Moons: 1
• Day length: 24 hours
• Average temperature: 15°C

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun.
Key Data:
• Moons: 0
• Day length: 59 Earth days
• Temperature: -180°C to +430°C

Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet. It has a dry surface and the largest volcano in the Solar System.
Key Data:
• Moons: 2 (Phobos & Deimos)
• Day length: 24.6 hours
• Temperature: -63°C

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its beautiful ring system made of ice and rock.
Key Data:
• Moons: 83
• Day length: 10.7 hours
• Temperature: -138°C

Uranus

Uranus is an icy planet that rotates on its side. Its pale blue color comes from methane in its atmosphere.
Key Data:
• Moons: 27
• Day length: 17 hours
• Temperature: -195°C
